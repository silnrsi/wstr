Tolaki (LBW) is a Celebic language (Austronesian › Malayo-Polynesian › Celebic) spoken by over 330,000 people in Southeast Sulawesi province, central Indonesia (see entry in Ethnologue). This data is for the Konawe and Mekongga dialacts, thus covering nearly 100% of the language community.

The phonemic inventory is taken from _Tolaki Phonology_ by David Mead and Tambunan (unpublished typescript, 1993), and _Tolaki Phonology_ by Owen Edwards (draft, 2013). The orthography was developed and tested by Scott Youngman (1995-2013).

Where Mead and Tambunan's phonemic analysis differs from Edwards, I have followed the former and made a note (applies to graphemes ‹<span lang='xxx-Latn'>b</span>›, ‹<span lang='xxx-Latn'>d</span>›, ‹<span lang='xxx-Latn'>e</span>›, and ‹<span lang='xxx-Latn'>w</span>›).
