## Script description

The Multani script was used from the 18th to the early 20th century for writing the Saraiki language, spoken in the Punjab regions of India and Pakistan, and in northern Sindh in Pakistan.

<details>
<summary>Read the full description...</summary>It was a commercial script, used mainly by merchants. Structurally, the script has characteristics of an abjad; vowels are generally not written unless they appear at the start of a word or in one-syllable V or CV clusters.
</details>
