The data shown below of graphemes and phonemes for English is quite simplified, and is intended to represent the Received Pronunciation of the UK and most North American dialects. A number of phonological issues, such as r-colored vowels (-er, -ar) and vowel length, are not included. Note also that the sounds indicated are phonemes, not actual phones, and as such do not reflect many subtleties of pronunciation. Also, only a small sample of words with unusual spellings have been included.

A common phenomenon in English orthography is the vowel + consonant + silent e sequence (e.g., 'bake', 'home') where the presence of the silent e affects the pronunciation of the vowel. This is not represented in this data.

One of the sources used in compiling this data was _The Writing Road to Reading_ by Romalda Bishop Spalding and Walter T. Spalding.
