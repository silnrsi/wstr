The 'Kurukh Banna' script was invented by 'Basudev Ram Khalkho' in Odisha during the 1990s to write the Kurukh language with ease. Kurukh is widely spoken by Oraon Tribal Community, mainly in India but also in Bangladesh and Bhutan. At present this script is being used by Oraon people in Odisha state. In states with fewer Oraon people, such as Chhattisgarh, Bengal, Jharkhand and Assam, some have started to use this script in their daily life.

In Sundargarh district of Odisha, 'Kurukh Banna' script is being taught and promoted by 'Kurukh Parha' which is a traditional and customary institution of Oraon tribal community in Odisha. To help with this, a font has been developed for the 'Kurukh Banna' script. Since then the community has started using it widely in publishing books, magazines, wedding cards etcetera.

Before the computer font was created, the villagers of Sundargarh, Odisha, were using the letters of this script on wall painting as part of their tribal art. 20 years have passed since then, and now the 'Kurukh Banna' script is used and practiced in more than 7 states in India.

Basudev Ram Khalkho has created more than 300 different designs of 'Kurukh Banna' scripts which can now be represented using a TrueType font, which is gaining popularity among the Oraon people in India.  However there is still a lack of opportunity as the community has been unable to find a route to get the script added to Unicode .

When there is Unicode recognition of this script, the Oraon Tribal people will be benefited not only in terms of technology but also in global acknowledgement of the existence of their art which will then be recognised as a proper script. 

Research on this unique script (Kurukh Banna) is very much needed .
