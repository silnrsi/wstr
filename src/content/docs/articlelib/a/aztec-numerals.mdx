The Aztec numerical system was  [vigesimal](/reference/glossary#vigesimala). Quantities up to twenty were indicated by the requisite number of dots. A flat line was used to indicate twenty, repeating it for quantities up to four hundred, while a sign like a fir tree signified four hundred. The next unit, eight thousand, was indicated by a bag, which referred to the almost innumerable contents of a sack of cacao beans.
