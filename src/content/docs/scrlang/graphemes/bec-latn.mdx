The data shown below represents the graphemes and phonemes for Iceve-Maci. In particular, it represents the Oliti (or Ici) dialect, from which the examples are drawn, although the Bacheve dialect differs very little.

The sounds indicated are phonemes, not actual phones. The phonemes /s/, /ts/ and /dz/ each have a palatalised allophone (resp. [ʃ], [tʃ] and [dʒ]) that occurs before certain dialect-dependent vowels that include at least /i/, /e/ and /ia/. Both /ɰ/ and /v/ are often reduced to one of the approximants [ɰ], [w] or [j], conditioned by the preceding vowel, particularly when they occur in noun suffixes. These are treated as allophones of /ɰ/ and /v/, despite the same phones also occurring in the phonemes /w/ and /j/. Simiarly, the realisation of /a/ when it occurs in prefixes has several realisations, some of which resemble other phonemes, such as /e/.

Vowels may be doubled in writing Iceve. This represents a sequence of two short vowels, rather than a separate long vowel phoneme. It is not separately illustrated in the data.

Diacritics are used to represent grammatical tone. This is not represented in the data.

See _Iceve Orthography_ (Cox 2023) and _A Phonological Overview of Iceve-Maci_ (Cox 2013) for further details.
