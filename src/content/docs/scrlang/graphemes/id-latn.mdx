This is a straightforward coverage of the standard dialect of Indonesian as taught formally, used in speeches, etc.

Other dialects tend to vary phonetically in fairly simple ways. (E.g. with a heavy Javanese accent, the affricates may be dental rather than alveolar, and voiced stops are extra long.)

_Disclaimer:_ The inventory of diphthongs is tentative (off the cuff) and is not a serious claim in favor of diphthongs. Most adjacent vowels are probably just sequences.
