This data is for Modern Standard Arabic (MSA).

Sources:

- [Wikipedia: Arabic, “Literary Arabic”](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic#Literary_Arabic)
- [Wikipedia: Hamza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamza)
- Daniels, Peter T. and William Bright, eds. 1996. _The World's Writing Systems_, p. 560
- Mace, John. 2003. _Beginner's Arabic Script_.

We did not include gemination over all consonants. It is likely that shadda (for gemination) can occur over other consonants. However, the use of shadda is rare in MSA and examples over other consonants proved difficult to find.

This information is purely a start on documenting the phonemes for MSA. We welcome feedback.
