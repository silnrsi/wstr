The _Shadda_ character is used for representing [geminate](/reference/glossary#gemination) (long) consonants. _Nukta_ is used to change the phonetic value of a letter so that it can represent non-native (primarily Arabic) sounds. For example, _ja_ + _nukta_ represents [za]. Both Shadda and Nukta are written above the consonant to which they apply, in which space certain vowel diacritics may also be written. (_Nukta_ may also be written above a vowel.) In the case where more than one sign needs to be written above a consonant, complex positioning must be applied as follows.<br />
Vowel sign _e_ or _ai_ with _shadda_ -&gt; _shadda_ is written to the right of the vowel sign.<br />
_Nukta_ with _shadda_ -&gt; _shadda_ is written below _nukta_.<br />
Vowel sign _e_ or _ai_ with _nukta_ -&gt; _nukta_ is written to the right of the vowel sign.
