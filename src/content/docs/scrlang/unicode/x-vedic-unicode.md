The Vedic Extensions block was first encoded in The Unicode Standard version 5.2. Vedic Extensions are discussed in {link:http://www.unicode.org/versions/latest/ch12.pdf Chapter 12 South and Central Asia-I: Official Scripts of India} in the Devanagari Extended section. Vedic Extensions may be used with many Indic scripts. Unicode Status pages for scripts that use the Vedic Extensions are found here: {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/f8jrlqwbac Bengali}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/b6c6j63sf5 Devanagari}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/qzhwzbsvm7 Grantha}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/yqug2kbjjr Gujarati}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/fd27tquj5j Gurmukhi}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/ur8y3qj6yk Kannada}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/ypvmjurtyg Malayalam}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/y53lggbts6 Newa}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/znkmblg693 Oriya}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/spnpfarl4t Sharada}, {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/zw5nedjarb Tamil} and {link:https://scriptsource.org/entry/sjrwm9ppr3 Telugu}.
